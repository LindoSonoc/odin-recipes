Isinkwa Sembila (Maize Bread)

By: Leendor | Updated on April 28, 2026

Isinkwa sembila is a traditional South African maize bread made from mealie meal. It is soft, filling, and often served with vegetables, stew, or sour milk.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups maize meal (mealie meal)
  • 2 1/2 cups warm water
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder (optional for softness)
  • 1 tablespoon oil or butter (optional)

Preparation Method : 45–60 Minutes

  1. Mix dry ingredients — In a bowl, combine maize meal, salt, sugar, and baking powder.
  2. Add water gradually and stir until you get a thick, smooth batter.
  3. Rest mixture for about 10–15 minutes so the maize absorbs water properly.
  4. Prepare steaming pot by boiling water in a large pot with a lid.
  5. Steam the mixture in a greased bowl or wrapped cloth for 40–50 minutes.
  6. Check doneness by inserting a skewer — it should come out clean.
  7. Serve warm with stew, vegetables, or milk.
